Topic Overview - Quadratics (Minimum, Maximum, Solving)

To find the minimum or maximum of a graph, complete the square, converting the equation \(y=ax^2+bx+c\) into an equation of the form \(y=a(x-h)^2+k\). Do note that \(h=\frac b{2a}\) and \(k=c-h^2\)

Quadratic relationships can be solved by factoring its terms into \((x-a)(x-b)\) and by using the zero product property, noting that \(x-a=0\) or \(x-b=0\). They can also be solved using the quadratic formula, \(x=\frac{-b\pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\). In general, graphing a quadratic function will use the x-intercepts, the axis of symmetry, the vertex, and the y-intercept.
