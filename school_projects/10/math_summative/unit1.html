Topic Overview - Analytical Geometry

To rationalize a denominator, multiply both sides by the conjugate of the denominator, which should cancel out radicals.

The midpoint of a line segment is the average of the x and y values. Its length is given by the pythagorean theorem, taking the square root of the sum of the squares of the x difference and y difference.

A circle is also given by the pythagorean theorem, where the distance from each point to the origin is constant. It is of the form x2+y2=r2, where r is the radius.
