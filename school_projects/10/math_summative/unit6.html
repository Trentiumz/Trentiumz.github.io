Topic Overview - Trigonometry

Similar triangles have equivalent corresponding angles and the same ratio of sides. The scale factor is this ratio, often denoted by k, and the area has a ratio of 1:k2. Opposite angles are equal, supplementary angles sum to 180 degrees and complementary angles sum to 90 degrees.

In a right triangle, the tangent ratio of an angle is the opposite side divided by the adjacent side. The ratio is constant and can be found for any valid angle given a calculator. Sine and Cosine are similar, but are opposite/hypotenuse and adjacent/hypotenuse, respectively. This can be remembered by SOH-CAH-TOA.

The angle of elevation is the angle above the horizontal and the angle of declination is the angle below the horizontal.
